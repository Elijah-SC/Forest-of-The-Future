Pines are evergreen trees with needle-like leaves and cones. They grow tall with straight trunks and are known for their aromatic wood. They thrive in a range of climates and are common in forests.

Birches are deciduous trees with white, peeling bark and slender trunks. They grow in temperate and boreal forests, often in well-drained soils.

Oaks are sturdy hardwood trees with lobed leaves and acorns. They have strong wood and are widespread across the Northern Hemisphere, thriving in various climates.

Redwoods are towering evergreens with thick, fibrous bark. Known for their incredible height, they thrive in the coastal regions of northern California and southern Oregon.

Sequoias are massive evergreen trees with reddish-brown bark and are among the largest by volume. They grow in the Sierra Nevada Mountains and are known for their immense size and longevity.

Willows are flexible trees or shrubs with long, slender branches and leaves. They prefer moist soils near water and are used for erosion control.
